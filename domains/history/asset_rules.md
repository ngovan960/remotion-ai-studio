# History Asset Rules

## Image Assets

### Required Types
- **Archival Photographs**: Historical images from the period
- **Period Artwork**: Paintings, sketches, engravings
- **Historical Maps**: Period-appropriate cartography
- **Documents**: Letters, newspapers, official records
- **Artifacts**: Physical objects, tools, clothing
- **Portraits**: Historical figures relevant to events

### Image Quality Standards
- Minimum resolution: 1920x1080
- Color profile: Period-appropriate (sepia, black & white)
- Format: PNG or high-quality JPEG
- No watermarks or logos
- Consistent style within time period

### Image Sources
- Historical archives (Library of Congress, British Library)
- Museum collections (digital archives)
- Academic databases
- Public domain sources
- Generated period-appropriate illustrations

## Video Assets

### Stock Footage Guidelines
- License: Royalty-free or Creative Commons
- Resolution: 4K preferred, 1080p minimum
- Frame rate: 24-30 fps
- Style: Period-appropriate aesthetics
- Subject: Historical locations, artifacts, reenactments

### Generated Video
- Timeline animations with Ken Burns effect
- Map animations showing territorial changes
- Chart animations revealing data
- Text animations for dates and facts
- Period-appropriate visual effects

## Timeline Assets

### Timeline Elements
- **Date markers**: Clear, readable dates
- **Event icons**: Relevant symbols for events
- **Connection lines**: Show relationships between events
- **Highlight effects**: Emphasize key moments
- **Zoom regions**: Allow focus on specific periods

### Timeline Styles
- **Linear**: Simple chronological progression
- **Branching**: Multiple parallel timelines
- **Comparative**: Side-by-side timelines
- **Interactive**: User-controlled exploration

## Map Assets

### Map Elements
- **Borders**: Period-accurate political boundaries
- **Labels**: Historical place names
- **Markers**: Key locations and events
- **Routes**: Movement of armies, trade, people
- **Layers**: Different types of information

### Map Styles
- **Political**: Shows governments and borders
- **Geographical**: Physical features and terrain
- **Thematic**: Specific topics (climate, population)
- **Animated**: Changes over time

## Chart Assets

### Chart Elements
- **Data points**: Accurate historical data
- **Labels**: Clear descriptions
- **Legends**: Explain symbols and colors
- **Animations**: Reveal data progressively
- **Annotations**: Explain significance

### Chart Types
- **Bar**: Compare quantities
- **Line**: Show trends over time
- **Pie**: Show proportions
- **Scatter**: Show relationships
- **Infographic**: Visual storytelling

## Audio Assets

### Music Selection
- **Period Music**: Music from the historical era
- **Cultural Instruments**: Traditional instruments
- **Ambient Historical**: Background sounds of period
- **Modern Interpretations**: Contemporary versions of period music

### Sound Effects
- **Period Sounds**: Authentic sounds of the era
- **Map Sounds**: Geographic and movement sounds
- **Data Sounds**: Chart and timeline sounds
- **Transitions**: Era-appropriate transitions

## Text Assets

### Typography
- **Font**: Period-appropriate styles
- **Size**: Readable at all scales
- **Color**: Era-appropriate palettes
- **Animation**: Subtle, period-appropriate

### Text Content Rules
- **Accuracy**: Fact-checked historical information
- **Dates**: Consistent format throughout
- **Names**: Proper historical spelling
- **Context**: Provide necessary background
- **Citations**: Source documentation

## Asset Organization

### Directory Structure
```
assets/
├── images/
│   ├── archival/
│   ├── portraits/
│   ├── maps/
│   └── artifacts/
├── video/
│   ├── stock/
│   └── generated/
├── timelines/
│   ├── elements/
│   └── templates/
├── maps/
│   ├── base_maps/
│   └── overlays/
├── charts/
│   ├── templates/
│   └── data/
├── audio/
│   ├── period_music/
│   ├── modern_music/
│   └── sfx/
└── text/
    ├── dates/
    ├── labels/
    └── annotations/
```

### Naming Convention
```
[domain]_[era]_[type]_[subject]_[variant].[ext]
Example: history_roman_map_empire_expansion_01.png
```
