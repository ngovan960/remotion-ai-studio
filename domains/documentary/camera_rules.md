# Documentary Camera Rules

## Movement Philosophy
Documentary camera work should feel observational, not intrusive. Movements should be motivated by the story and support the narrative flow.

## Allowed Movements

### Static Shots
- **Use for**: Interviews, important details, text overlays
- **Duration**: 5-15 seconds
- **Purpose**: Stability, focus, clarity

### Slow Pan
- **Speed**: 0.3x (30% of normal speed)
- **Use for**: Establishing shots, revealing landscapes, following action
- **Range**: 30-90 degrees maximum
- **Purpose**: Gentle exploration, context building

### Slow Zoom
- **Speed**: 0.2x (20% of normal speed)
- **Use for**: Emphasizing details, emotional moments, transitions
- **Range**: 10-30% zoom range
- **Purpose**: Drawing attention, creating intimacy

### Dolly/Track
- **Speed**: 0.25x
- **Use for**: Following subjects, exploring spaces
- **Distance**: 5-20 meters
- **Purpose**: Immersion, spatial awareness

### Aerial/Drone
- **Speed**: 0.15x
- **Use for**: Establishing location, scale, context
- **Height**: 50-200 meters
- **Purpose**: Overview, grandeur, geography

## Camera Angles

### Eye Level
- **Default for**: Interviews, conversations, neutral observation
- **Psychology**: Equality, directness, honesty

### High Angle
- **Use for**: Overview, context, vulnerability
- **Avoid**: Overuse, condescension

### Low Angle
- **Use for**: Power, importance, drama
- **Avoid**: Exaggeration, hero-worship

### Dutch Angle
- **NEVER USE**: Documentary should maintain objectivity

## Transition Rules

### Crossfade
- **Duration**: 1-2 seconds
- **Use for**: Time passage, scene changes, emotional shifts
- **Purpose**: Smooth, unobtrusive

### Dip to Black
- **Duration**: 0.5-1 second
- **Use for**: Chapter breaks, significant time jumps
- **Purpose**: Clear separation

### Cut
- **Use for**: Same location, continuous action, interview edits
- **Avoid**: Jarring or confusing

### Wipe
- **NEVER USE**: Too flashy for documentary style

## Composition Rules

### Rule of Thirds
- **Apply to**: All shots unless intentionally centered
- **Subject placement**: Intersection points
- **Horizon**: Upper or lower third line

### Leading Lines
- **Use for**: Drawing eye to subject, creating depth
- **Elements**: Roads, fences, architecture, natural lines

### Framing
- **Use for**: Emphasis, context, isolation
- **Elements**: Doorways, windows, arches, branches

### Negative Space
- **Use for**: Breathing room, emphasis, mood
- **Amount**: 30-50% of frame

## Forbidden Techniques
- Handheld shake (except intentional verite)
- Rapid zooms or pans
- 360-degree rotations
- Drone fly-throughs (too cinematic)
- Rack focus (distracting)
- Slow motion (except specific historical recreations)

## Scene-Specific Rules

### Interview Setup
- **Camera**: Static, eye level
- **Framing**: Medium close-up (head and shoulders)
- **Background**: Relevant but not distracting
- **Lighting**: Natural, soft, flattering

### B-Roll Footage
- **Camera**: Slow pan or static
- **Framing**: Wide to medium shots
- **Content**: Supporting visuals for narration
- **Duration**: 5-10 seconds per clip

### Establishing Shots
- **Camera**: Slow pan or aerial
- **Framing**: Wide shot
- **Content**: Location, context, atmosphere
- **Duration**: 8-15 seconds

### Detail Shots
- **Camera**: Static or slow zoom
- **Framing**: Close-up
- **Content**: Important objects, text, faces
- **Duration**: 4-8 seconds
