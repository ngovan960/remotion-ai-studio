# Documentary Audio Rules

## Audio Philosophy
Audio should support and enhance the narration without overpowering it. The goal is to create an immersive experience that feels authentic and emotionally resonant.

## Voiceover Requirements

### Technical Standards
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 16-bit or 24-bit
- **Format**: WAV or high-quality MP3 (320 kbps)
- **Volume**: -3dB to -6dB (loud enough to be clear)

### Performance Guidelines
- **Pace**: Moderate, allow breathing room
- **Tone**: Authoritative but approachable
- **Emotion**: Match the content's gravity
- **Clarity**: Every word must be intelligible

### Recording Quality
- **Environment**: Quiet, treated room
- **Equipment**: Professional microphone
- **Editing**: Remove breaths, pauses, mistakes
- **Processing**: Light compression, EQ, de-essing

## Background Music

### Selection Criteria
- **Genre**: Ambient, orchestral, piano, acoustic guitar
- **Mood**: Contemplative, hopeful, serious, nostalgic
- **Tempo**: 60-80 BPM for narration, 90-120 for montages
- **Key**: Minor for serious, major for hopeful

### Volume Levels
- **With Voiceover**: -12dB to -18dB
- **Without Voiceover**: -6dB to -9dB
- **Transitions**: Fade in/out over 2-3 seconds

### Music Structure
- **Intro**: 5-10 seconds before narration
- **Body**: Loop or extend to match scene length
- **Outro**: Fade out 3-5 seconds before scene ends
- **Transitions**: Crossfade between tracks

## Sound Effects

### Allowed Types
- **Nature**: Wind, water, birds, insects, rain
- **Urban**: Traffic, crowds, machinery, footsteps
- **Period**: Historical sounds when available
- **Transitions**: Subtle whooshes, risers, impacts

### Volume Levels
- **Background**: -18dB to -24dB
- **Featured**: -12dB to -15dB
- **Never overpower voiceover**

### Timing
- **Sync with visuals**: Match actions precisely
- **Anticipation**: 0.5-1 second before visual
- **Decay**: Natural fade out

## Audio Transitions

### Crossfade
- **Duration**: 1-3 seconds
- **Use for**: Scene changes, mood shifts
- **Curve**: Linear or equal power

### Fade In/Out
- **Duration**: 2-5 seconds
- **Use for**: Beginning/end of scenes
- **Curve**: Exponential (natural sounding)

### Hard Cut
- **Use for**: Same location, continuous audio
- **Avoid**: Between different music tracks

## Scene-Specific Audio

### Interview Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Primary focus, clear and present
- **Music**: Minimal, ambient, supportive
- **SFX**: None unless relevant to content

### B-Roll Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Continues from interview
- **Music**: Slightly more prominent
- **SFX**: Relevant ambient sounds

### Montage Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Minimal or none
- **Music**: Primary element, emotional
- **SFX**: Supporting visual actions

### Transition Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Bridges between topics
- **Music**: Crossfades between tracks
- **SFX**: Subtle transition sounds

## Audio Mixing

### Levels Hierarchy
1. Voiceover (-3dB to -6dB)
2. Music (-12dB to -18dB)
3. SFX (-18dB to -24dB)
4. Ambient (-24dB to -30dB)

### EQ Guidelines
- **Voiceover**: Cut below 80Hz, boost 2-5kHz
- **Music**: Cut below 60Hz, slight boost 100-200Hz
- **SFX**: Match to content, avoid mud

### Compression
- **Voiceover**: 3:1 ratio, fast attack
- **Music**: 2:1 ratio, medium attack
- **SFX**: 4:1 ratio, fast attack

## Quality Control

### Technical Checks
- **Clipping**: No digital distortion
- **Noise**: No background hum or hiss
- **Consistency**: Levels match throughout
- **Sync**: Audio aligns with visuals

### Creative Checks
- **Emotion**: Audio supports story
- **Pacing**: Music matches scene rhythm
- **Balance**: No element overpowers another
- **Transitions**: Smooth between scenes

## Forbidden Audio
- Pop music with lyrics (distracting)
- Heavy electronic beats (too modern)
- Dramatic orchestral stabs (too theatrical)
- Sound effects that contradict visuals
- Music that tells viewer how to feel
