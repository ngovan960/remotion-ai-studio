# Horror Story Audio Rules

## Audio Philosophy
Horror audio is the primary fear-building tool. Sound should create unease, build tension, and deliver impact while leaving space for imagination.

## Voiceover Requirements

### Technical Standards
- **Sample Rate**: 48 kHz
- **Bit Depth**: 24-bit
- **Format**: WAV or high-quality MP3
- **Volume**: -6dB to -12dB (intimate, close)

### Performance Guidelines
- **Pace**: Slow, deliberate, with pauses
- **Tone**: Whispered, urgent, fearful
- **Emotion**: Genuine terror, not theatrical
- **Clarity**: Every word must be chilling
- **Breathing**: Audible, adds realism

### Recording Quality
- **Environment**: Quiet, intimate space
- **Equipment**: Close-mic'd condenser
- **Editing**: Minimal, preserve raw emotion
- **Processing**: Light compression, subtle reverb

## Background Music

### Selection Criteria
- **Genre**: Dark ambient, horror soundscapes
- **Mood**: Dread, tension, unease
- **Tempo**: Slow, building (40-70 BPM)
- **Key**: Dissonant, minor, atonal

### Volume Levels
- **Building Tension**: -12dB to -18dB
- **Peak Horror**: -6dB to -9dB
- **Silence**: Absolute (-∞dB)
- **Jump Scare**: 0dB to -3dB (sudden)

### Music Structure
- **Intro**: Subtle, establishing dread
- **Build**: Gradual increase in intensity
- **Peak**: Maximum fear moment
- **Release**: Brief, false safety
- **Outro**: Lingering unease

## Sound Effects

### Ambient Sounds
- **Creaking**: Wood, metal, doors
- **Wind**: Through windows, empty spaces
- **Dripping**: Water, steady rhythm
- **Breathing**: Character's, or unseen entity
- **Heartbeat**: Building, accelerating

### Horror Elements
- **Whispers**: Unintelligible, directionless
- **Footsteps**: Approaching, retreating
- **Scratching**: Walls, floors, furniture
- **Impacts**: Sudden, loud, jarring
- **Silence**: Absolute, oppressive

### Volume Levels
- **Background Ambient**: -24dB to -30dB
- **Featured SFX**: -12dB to -18dB
- **Jump Scare**: 0dB to -3dB
- **Aftermath**: -30dB to -40dB

## Audio Transitions

### Crossfade
- **Duration**: 3-5 seconds
- **Use for**: Scene changes, mood shifts
- **Curve**: Exponential (natural)

### Fade In/Out
- **Duration**: 5-10 seconds
- **Use for**: Building tension
- **Curve**: Slow, deliberate

### Hard Cut
- **Use for**: Jump scares, shock
- **Duration**: Instant
- **Effect**: Maximum impact

### Silence
- **Duration**: 2-5 seconds
- **Use for**: Anticipation, false safety
- **Effect**: Heightened awareness

## Scene-Specific Audio

### Atmosphere Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Minimal, whispered
- **Music**: Subtle ambient, building
- **SFX**: Environmental, continuous

### Build Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Urgent, growing fear
- **Music**: Increasing intensity
- **SFX**: Approaching, layering

### Jump Scare Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Scream or gasp
- **Music**: Sudden impact
- **SFX**: Loud, shocking

### Aftermath Scenes
- **Voiceover**: Shaky, breathing hard
- **Music**: Fading, lingering
- **SFX**: Distant, unresolved

## Audio Mixing

### Levels Hierarchy
1. Jump Scares (0dB)
2. Voiceover (-6dB to -12dB)
3. Featured SFX (-12dB to -18dB)
4. Music (-18dB to -24dB)
5. Ambient (-24dB to -30dB)

### EQ Guidelines
- **Voiceover**: Boost 2-5kHz for intimacy
- **Music**: Cut below 60Hz, boost 100-200Hz
- **SFX**: Match to content, avoid mud
- **Ambience**: Low-pass filter, distant

### Compression
- **Voiceover**: 3:1 ratio, fast attack
- **Music**: 2:1 ratio, medium attack
- **SFX**: 4:1 ratio, fast attack
- **Jump Scares**: Limit to prevent clipping

## Quality Control

### Technical Checks
- **Clipping**: No digital distortion
- **Noise**: No unwanted background
- **Consistency**: Levels match throughout
- **Sync**: Audio aligns with visuals

### Creative Checks
- **Fear Factor**: Does it scare?
- **Tension**: Does it build?
- **Impact**: Does it shock?
- **Aftermath**: Does it linger?

## Forbidden Audio
- Pop music (breaks immersion)
- Excessive gore sounds (gratuitous)
- Predictable horror stings (cliché)
- Over-explanation (reduces fear)
- Happy music (wrong tone)
